# Colorado AI Act § 6-1-1701(9) — High-Risk AI Systems

A "high-risk artificial intelligence system" means any artificial intelligence system that, when deployed, makes, or is a substantial factor in making, a consequential decision.

## Consequential Decision Definition

A "consequential decision" means any decision that has a material legal or similarly significant effect on the provision or denial to, or the cost or terms of:
- Education enrollment or an educational opportunity
- Employment or an employment opportunity
- A financial or lending service
- An essential government service
- Healthcare services
- Housing
- Insurance
- Legal services

## Substantial Factor

A "substantial factor" means a factor generated by an artificial intelligence system that is used to assist in making, and is capable of altering the outcome of, a consequential decision.

## Exclusions

"High-risk artificial intelligence system" does not include an artificial intelligence system that is intended to perform a narrow procedural task or detect decision-making patterns or deviations from prior decision-making patterns and make no recommendation.

The following technologies are also excluded from the definition of "high-risk artificial intelligence system" unless the technology is used to make, or is a substantial factor in making, a consequential decision:
- Anti-fraud technology that does not use facial recognition technology
- Anti-malware, anti-virus, and firewall technology
- Artificial intelligence used solely for the purpose of video game development
- Calculators
- Cybersecurity applications and infrastructure
- Databases and data storage applications and infrastructure
- Internet domain registration and website hosting
- Spell-checking applications
- Spreadsheets
- Web caching or web hosting
- Chatbots subject to an acceptable use policy that prohibits generating content that discriminates or is harmful
